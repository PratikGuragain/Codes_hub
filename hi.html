Every prime number >(greater than)2 %(modulo)3 can be written in the form 4n+1 or 4n+3


Proof


Any number p when / (divided by) 4 leaves remainder 0, 1, or 2. Therefore, the number has the form 4n, 4n + 1, or 4n+ 2. Since 4n and 4n + 2 are even, they can not be a form of a prime number which is an odd number (except 2).
